# Saint Carlo Acutis

**Born:** May 3, 1991, London, England (to Italian parents; raised in Milan)
**Died:** October 12, 2006, Monza, Italy — acute promyelocytic leukemia, age 15
**Beatified:** October 10, 2020, by Pope Francis (Assisi)
**Canonized:** April 27, 2025 — the first millennial saint
**Feast day:** October 12
**Tomb / shrine:** Sanctuary of the Renunciation, Assisi, Italy (body publicly venerated since 2019)

## Life

Carlo Acutis was an Italian teenager known for his intense Eucharistic devotion and his pioneering use of the internet to spread the Catholic faith. From an early age he attended daily Mass — calling the Eucharist his "highway to heaven" — prayed the Rosary, and went to weekly confession. He was an ordinary student in many respects but stood out for his charity toward the homeless and his quiet evangelization of classmates and family. His mother, Antonia Salzano, has publicly credited Carlo with her own return to the practice of the faith.

A self-taught computer programmer, Carlo used his technical skills to catalog accounts of Eucharistic miracles and Marian apparitions from around the world.

## The Eucharistic Miracles Exhibition (the catalog)

Between roughly 2002 and 2006, Carlo designed and built a website and traveling exhibition documenting more than 100 Vatican-recognized or locally-approved Eucharistic miracles spanning over 1,300 years of Church history. Each miracle is presented on a panel with location, date, historical narrative, photographs, and references.

After his death, his work has been continued by the Associazione Amici di Carlo Acutis (Friends of Carlo Acutis Association), which administers the original site at **miracolieucaristici.org** and licenses the traveling exhibit to dioceses, parishes, and Catholic schools worldwide. The exhibit has appeared in thousands of locations across all six inhabited continents and is one of the most widely circulated Catholic catechetical resources of the 21st century.

## Cause of canonization

- **Heroic virtue** declared by Pope Francis in 2018 (titled "Venerable").
- **First miracle (for beatification):** healing of Matheus Vianna, a Brazilian boy born with a congenital pancreatic defect (Annular Pancreas), October 2013, attributed to Carlo's intercession.
- **Beatification:** October 10, 2020, in Assisi.
- **Second miracle (for canonization):** healing of Valeria Valverde, a Costa Rican student in Florence with a serious traumatic brain injury (2022).
- **Canonization:** April 27, 2025 — declared the first saint of the millennial generation.

He is widely referred to as **"God's Influencer"** and **"the Patron of the Internet."**

## Theological significance

Carlo's witness ties together two threads central to TrueCatholic AI's mission:

1. **The Eucharist as the source and summit of Christian life.** His phrase *"the Eucharist is my highway to heaven"* is among the most quoted devotional lines from a contemporary saint.
2. **The faithful use of digital tools to evangelize.** Carlo demonstrated that technology — including the internet — is morally neutral and can be ordered to the worship of God and the salvation of souls when used in fidelity to the Church.
