Questions

What's the difference between Free and paid plans?
The doctrine is the same. Every plan runs the full 2,700-line charter. The difference is the AI model underneath. Free runs Claude Haiku — fast and faithful, but less capable on complex reasoning. Paid plans unlock frontier models like Sonnet, GPT-4o, and Opus that handle nuanced theology, homework help, essay writing, and deep apologetics questions significantly better. Think of it like the same teacher using a whiteboard vs. a full classroom — same knowledge, better tools.
Can I really use it without an account?
Yes. The free tier requires nothing — no email, no signup, no tracking. You go to the site and start asking. We don't store your conversations. We don't build a profile on you. We don't sell data. If you want the paid features, then you'll create an account — but the faith is never behind a wall.
Is this safe for my kids?
That's why we built it. The charter includes explicit guidance on age-appropriate responses, pastoral sensitivity, and content boundaries. Unlike secular AI, it won't undermine your teaching, affirm things that contradict Church doctrine, or fold under pressure when your teenager pushes back on a hard moral question. The Family plan adds a parent topic digest so you can see what subjects your kids are exploring — not surveillance, just awareness.
What if a competitor builds something better?
We'd celebrate. The goal is faithful Catholic AI, not market dominance. We'll benchmark every Catholic AI that launches on the Catholic Faithfulness Index and publish the results — even if they beat us. Our charter is public specifically so others can learn from it. If someone builds a better Catholic AI, Catholics win.
How does the Family plan work?
One account, up to 6 seats. Each family member gets their own login and conversation history. Parents can set age-appropriate modes for younger children and receive a weekly digest of the topics their kids asked about (not transcripts — just topics like "asked about Confession" or "discussed evolution and faith"). Parents get full access to all premium models. Children default to Sonnet with tighter content boundaries.
Where does my money go?
API costs (paying the AI providers for compute), infrastructure (servers, hosting), and keeping the free tier free. We're a small operation — a Catholic father building this as a vocation. There's no venture capital, no investor pressure to monetize your data, and no incentive to compromise doctrine for growth. Patron subscriptions directly subsidize free access for anyone who needs it.
Can I cancel anytime?
Yes, instantly. No retention tricks, no "are you sure?" gauntlet, no hidden fees. You can also downgrade to the free tier and keep asking questions about the faith — the charter is always running.
